USurf UX

Purpose

A surfing app that provides beautifully displayed and easy to understand wind, wave and weather reports, forecasts and statistics for surfers.

Objective

Surfers need a way to view surfing conditions because they don’t want to plan a day of surfing that gets ruined due to inclement weather, danger or by choosing the wrong location. We will know this to be true when we see that surfers are using this app consistently to find the best surfing conditions and locations.

Role

UX Designer

Duration

8 Months

My Process

Research

Once we had our  initial idea locked down we moved into our research phase. To better understand our audience we conducted user interviews with these three research goals in mind. 

  • 1. To better understand user behavior around surfing
  • 2. To determine which tasks users would like to complete before heading out to surf
  • 3. Documenting user pain points with existing surfing / weather apps.

Once the interviews concluded we used affinity mapping and cluster insights to sort through the data.

Design & Feel

Users want to see a visually appealing application that feels simple to use and not over crowed with information. 

Time

Users do not want to spend a lot of time in the app itself

Customization & Exploration

Users have preferences on what they consider good surfing conditions

Conditions & Accuracy

Users want accurate and up to date weather data

Personas

From our research we were able to make user personas to help keep our design focused on the user.

User Flows

Based on our research and personas we built user flows to discover what pages were needed for them to complete their desired task.

Wireframes

Once we understood our audience we began to work on designing the app. We began with low-fidelity wireframes and worked up to a high-fidelity clickable prototype. During this process we were able to test with users at every step which allowed us to continually better the app.

Final Prototype

All this lead to our clickable prototype. Please feel free to explore USurf!
